About Me

Hello, I'm Yaten-Z.

I believe that code and literature are fundamentally connected—both use structured language to express ideas. Frontend development allows me to transform imagination into interactive experiences, while classical literature leads me to contemplate deeper humanistic meanings. Moving between these two worlds, I've found my true passion.

On the technical side, I'm continuously learning new skills, from Python to C++, from HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to Linux. I believe good engineering practices enable creative ideas to materialize more efficiently. I've participated in several projects including anonymous message boards, personal blogs, and team websites—each one a journey of thoughtful refinement from scratch.

In terms of hobbies, I love music and copywriting. These passions have taught me how to observe the world—finding patterns, discovering contrasts, pursuing balance—qualities that are equally important in code design.

I'm part of the TATEN Team, a group of passionate computer science enthusiasts. We believe in "the romance of code"—every project is a testament to technology and creativity, a way to record our youthful passion. In this team, I've learned not just how to code, but also how to collaborate with others and turn ideas into reality.

I maintain constant curiosity about new things. Quirky creative ideas, unexplored technological domains, and interesting collaboration opportunities—these are the things that excite me. If you're interested in code, design, or any creative projects, I'd love to connect with you.

It's just finding one sentence with another, one person thinking about another.
But in this process, perhaps we can do something meaningful.
